## The Traffic Crisis: Zero-Click Is Eating the Open Web

The open web is facing its most serious threat in two decades. Search engines—once the primary traffic source for publishers—are sending fewer users to websites than ever before.

According to multiple industry studies:

* **59%** of U.S. Google searches now end in **zero-click** interactions.
* Google’s AI Overviews (SGE) reduce top-result CTR by **34.5%**.
* **35%** of marketers report significant organic traffic drops due to SGE.

This means users increasingly find answers **without visiting websites**. Publishers lose pageviews, ad impressions, affiliate clicks, and ultimately—**revenue**.

SEO isn’t dying. It’s collapsing.

---

## Why Zero-Click Is Devastating for Publishers

When users stop clicking, publishers lose the metrics that their business models depend on:

* Sessions
* Time on site
* Ad impressions
* Affiliate conversions
* Email signups

But the problem goes deeper than traffic—it affects **discoverability**. AI-generated answers summarize content in a way that:

* strips publisher credit,
* keeps users on Google’s page,
* and prevents websites from capturing value.

The internet has never been more centralized.

This is the *real* shift—**attention has moved from websites to conversations**.
